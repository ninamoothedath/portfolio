Informational Video: I created this informational motion graphic myself based off the fictional show 'Succession.' As I was creating this, I specifically tried to replicate the style of animation found in several Vox videos. After studying clips from these videos I also incorporated colors from the show to create the final informational video.

I created this illustration to accompany an article about the danger women face in rideshares. After reading the article, I brainstormed ideas, submitted thumbnails and rough sketches for approval from my editor, and made edits based on feedback for the final published illustration.

This was my entry to a creative magazine for the theme "Exposure". After brainstorming, I photographed a model and digitally compiled the results into my own reference photo before creating the final design.

Projects I've worked on include published stories and elements for Annenberg Media's Interactives desk, hackathon projects, and my own personal projects. The main tools I use are HTML, CSS, React, and jQuery.

Using the The Movie Database API, I developed a web application using React.js where users can search for movies and view the 20 most relevant results

I created an interactive story for an art show for viewers to scroll over the images included within the story to learn more about the artist of a specific piece and their inspiration or creation process. While an editor assisted me with the final layout, I coded all interactive elements myself, took all photos featured and did my own reporting and writing.

"Welcome to the Path" is a short choose-your-own-adventure web game with multiple endings based on players' choices. All coding for the game, along with graphics, was done myself.

For the hackathon HackSC, I created an educational website to encourage young students to learn about the solar system. I coded the project myself and designed all graphics displayed on the site using Illustrator.

GymGals was designed to help women in LA find others to go to the gym with to make space less intimidating. The sign-up forms are funcitonal, linked to add the user's information to a Google Sheets database. I worked on the main landing page's design and code, as well as the code connecting the website form to Google sheets.

My main multimedia work includes recording and editing broadcast and radio stories and working as video editor of The Rundown. The main tools I use are Adobe Premiere, Audition, After Effects and Photoshop. I've worked to develop content for various platforms that require different voices and formats, such as news broadcast, social media, and blogs. My ongoing projects include my current role as a podcast producer for the USC Information Sciences Insitute.

I've worked behind the scenes on multiple live broadcasts, including Annenberg TV News, Annenberg Radio News and The Water Cooler. I've collaborated with directors,producers and talent both as a floor manager and in control rooms working livestreams, cameras and sound. I've also designed live motion graphics like the one on the left, edited video sports highlight reels, moderated live chat questions, and supervised volunteers I trained in using broadcast software and equipment.

In addition to my work behind the scenes on live broadcasts, I worked on the set of multiple student films as a Boom Operator and PA. This included running wireless and boom microphone systems and collaborating with directors, crew and talent to capture dialogue in the highest quality possible.

I've used tools such as Python, Pandas, R, mySQL, Tableau and Neo4J to analyze, manipulate and visualize large datasets. Examples include databases with dependencies between customer, product and vendor databases, flight information, scientific data, and movie ratings.
